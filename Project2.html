Basic Linear Power Supply

The project involved the design and construction of a basic linear power supply. It included the creation of an unregulated AC-to-DC power supply with a regulated power supply circuit. The power supply circuit was designed and constructed using components such as a power transformer, rectifier diodes, filter capacitors, and resistors. The power transformer was responsible for reducing the AC voltage from the power source to a suitable level for further processing. To convert the AC input into pulsating DC, rectifier diodes were employed. The filter capacitors were used to smooth out the pulsations, resulting in a more stable DC output. Careful selection of resistor values ensured the circuit could handle the desired power output. To regulate the power supply, a regulator circuit was implemented. This circuit maintained a constant voltage output, even when the input voltage or current fluctuated. The effectiveness of the regulator was evaluated by measuring the voltage before and after its application. Testing and adjustment of the circuit were performed using digital multimeters and dual trace oscilloscopes. These instruments allowed for the measurement of voltage, current, and waveform characteristics, ensuring the proper function and performance of the power supply. The project provided an opportunity to gain a comprehensive understanding of rectifier diodes, filter capacitors, voltage regulation, and power resistors. This hands-on experience deepened my knowledge and practical skills related to power supply design and construction, with the potential for application in future projects or real-world scenarios.
